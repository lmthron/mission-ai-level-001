The Three-Question Decision Framework

Ask Yourself These Questions to Decide Whether to Use AI:

1. Is this task repetitive?
Yes ‚Üí Good for AI
No ‚Üí Maybe not worth setup
2. Would explaining take less time than doing?
Yes ‚Üí AI might help
No ‚Üí Just do it yourself
3. Can I verify the output easily?
Yes ‚Üí AI can help
No ‚Üí Too risky
Decision Rule: If all three are "Yes" ‚Üí Use AI. If two or more are "No" ‚Üí Skip AI.
When to Use AI vs. When to Skip It

‚úÖ AI Saves Time When:

  • Tasks are repetitive
  • Tasks are time-consuming but straightforward
  • You need multiple options
  • You're not sure how to start
  • Format matters more than perfect wording

‚ùå Skip AI When:

  • The task is quicker to do yourself
  • It requires specific internal knowledge*
  • It's personal/creative work where your voice matters
  • You're the only expert and can't verify

*We have Claude connected to some company systems through Connectors. For these systems, Claude CAN access internal information.

Custom Instructions - Set Preferences Once

What Are Custom Instructions?

Instead of explaining your preferences every time you use AI, set them once. AI remembers and applies them automatically to every conversation.

Purpose: Save time by teaching AI your working style and general preferences.

The Two-Part Structure

Part 1: About You
  • ‚Ä¢ Your role and context
  • ‚Ä¢ Who you work with
  • ‚Ä¢ The type of work you do
  • ‚Ä¢ Your typical audiences
Part 2: How to Respond
  • ‚Ä¢ Format preferences
  • ‚Ä¢ Tone (formal, casual, technical)
  • ‚Ä¢ When to ask clarifying questions
  • ‚Ä¢ Response length and style

What to Include (and Not Include)

‚úì Include: Broad preferences that apply to most of your AI conversations

‚úó Don't Include: Temporary projects, detailed task procedures, current priorities that change frequently
