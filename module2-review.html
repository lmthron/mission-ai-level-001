The Anatomy of a Good Prompt

Review the four-element framework and common mistakes before taking your Module 2 checkpoint quiz. Mastering prompting is the key to getting useful AI results.

The Four-Element Framework

1. Context

Tell AI what it needs to know to understand your request. What's the situation? Who's the audience? What's the purpose?

2. Specificity

Be clear about exactly what you want. What format? What length? What should be included?

3. Constraints

What should AI include or avoid? Set boundaries so AI doesn't make wrong assumptions.

4. Format

How should the output be structured? Specify length, style, and organization.

Remember:

When you provide these four things, AI can give you exactly what you need. The more specific and clear you are, the better the results.

Common Prompting Mistakes to Avoid
  • Mistake #1: Asking for everything at once - Instead: Break it into steps. First get a draft, then refine it.
  • Mistake #2: Not providing examples - Instead: Show what "nicely" or "professional" means with concrete examples.
  • Mistake #3: Assuming AI knows your context - Instead: Be explicit. AI doesn't know what "this" is or what "fixed" means.
  • Mistake #4: Giving up after bad output - Instead: Refine your prompt and try again. Bad output often means unclear instructions.
Refining Through Iteration

AI rarely gets it perfect on the first try. Learn to refine by telling AI specifically what to fix:

  • "This is too formal. Make it more conversational and friendly."
  • "This is good, but add a section about next steps and who's responsible for what."
  • "Make this half as long and focus only on the budget concerns, not the timeline."
