Understanding Large Language Models (LLMs)

The Autocomplete Analogy

Think of an LLM like autocomplete on steroids. Your phone predicts the next word when texting - LLMs do that with billions of examples to learn from.

The Library Analogy

Imagine someone who has read every book in a massive library but doesn't actually understand the world. They can tell you what words usually come after other words and what information typically appears together, but they're not "thinking" the way humans do. They're pattern matching at an incredibly sophisticated level.

What This Means for You

  • AI is very good at tasks involving language patterns (writing, summarizing, formatting, explaining)
  • AI doesn't "know" things the way you do (it can't fact-check itself or know what's true)
  • AI works best when you give it clear instructions and context
What AI Can Do Well
  • Summarize and synthesize information: "Take these 10 customer emails and give me the common themes"
  • Draft and edit text: "Write a professional response to this complaint email"
  • Explain complex concepts: "Explain blockchain like I'm a 10-year-old"
  • Generate options and ideas: "Give me 5 different subject lines for this email"
  • Format and restructure information: "Turn these notes into a bulleted list organized by priority"
  • Answer questions based on information you provide: "Based on this report, what were the top 3 concerns?"
What AI Cannot Do (Or Shouldn't)
  • Replace human judgment: AI doesn't know your company policies, your customer's history, or what's appropriate in your context
  • Guarantee accuracy: AI can confidently state wrong information (called "hallucinations"). Always verify important facts
  • Understand nuance and emotion perfectly: AI can miss sarcasm, cultural context, or the emotional weight of a situation
  • Make ethical decisions: AI will do what you ask. It's your job to decide if what you're asking is appropriate
